Smoked Salmon

Serves: 8   Prep Time: 8-10 hours

Ingredients

  • 1 lb salmon
  • 2 tbsp. kosher salt
  • 1 tbsp. pepper
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar

Instructions

  1. Remove scales and pin bones from salmon.
  2. Mix salmon, salt, pepper, and sugar. Let the salmon sit in the mixture for 4-8 hours.
  3. Rinse the salmon under cool water and pat dry.
  4. Add wood chips (hickory or similar) and drip pan with water to your smoker.
  5. Place salmon skin-side down onto smoker racks.
  6. Close smoker door and gradually increase the heat of the smoker until it reaches about 140 degrees.
  7. Let the salmon smoke for 2 hours at 140-175 degrees.
  8. Turn off the smoker and let the salmon air cool for a few minutes.

Suggested serving

  • Enjoy your smoked salmon by itself, as a main dish, or as a side with charcuterie.
  • Ready to take it to the next level? Try this creamy Smoked Salmon Dip.

Tips

  • Smoked Salmon is great for snacking.
  • Smoked Salmon will last up to 1 week wrapped in plastic in the fridge, up to 3 weeks in vaccum-seal, and up to a year in the freezer.
  • Vaccum-sealed smoked salmon makes a great addition to your backpack for longer outdoor excursions.
  • Take smoked salmon with you as a high-protein snack on your hiking, camping, or hunting trips.
