Blueberry Mango Kombucha

Serves: 8   Prep Time: 7-10 days

Ingredients

  • SCOBY
  • 6 cups Black tea
  • 1.5 cups sugar
  • 1 lb ripe blueberries (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 lb ripe mango (fresh or frozen)

Instructions

  1. Brew tea.
  2. Add tea, sugar, and water to a 1.5-gallon jar. The jar should be about 80% full, with space for the SCOBY plus about 1 inch of air at the top.
  3. Add scoby to lukewarm tea.
  4. Let scoby sit for 5-7 days. Make sure the scoby jar is covered with a breathable material such as a coffee filter or a clean towel.
  5. Place fruit in bottom of empty glass jars. You can use one empty gallon or eight 16oz jars.
  6. Remove the solid scoby from the first jar, then pour the liquid from the scoby jar into the jars with fruit.
  7. Screw the lids tightly onto the jars, and let them sit in a cool, dark place for 3 days.

Suggested serving

  • Enjoy as a cool refreshing drink on a hot summer's day, or as an early morning pick-me-up.
  • Kombucha is a great subsititute for soft drinks.It has a lower glycemic index than fruit juice, no artificial ingredients, and the good bacteria from the SCOBY gives your digestive system a boost. Win-win-win!
  • There is a small amount of naturally-occuring caffeine in Kombucha from the black tea. This makes kombucha an excellent substitute for coffee or other caffeinated beverages.
  • It's not recommended to drink Kombucha at night, unless you plan to stay awake late. The caffeine can keep your brain running much longer than it would otherwise.

Tips

  • SCOBY stands for 'Symbiotic Culture of Bacteria and Yeast'. This SCOBY is what forms the basis for your Kombucha.
  • The length of fermentation will vary based on the strength of the scoby and how strong you want your kombucha to be. Generally speaking, kombucha that has sat longer (e.g 7 days or more) will taste more like vinegar, while scoby that is less fermented (7 days or less) will taste sweeter.
  • Before you add the SCOBY to the tea, make sure the tea has cooled to lukewarm or room temperature. Too much heat can kill the SCOBY.
  • There is a small amount of alcohol in Kombucha. Most store-bought kombucha has less than 0.5% alcohol, which is why it can be listed as a non-alcoholic beverage.
  • There are variants of kombucha that have more alcohol. Enjoy responsibly - or not at all.
